This is the most significant issue found in the F2B run, and it is systemic
across the patch-based skills.

SKILLS.md §10 states: an edit — **SME *or* skill** — must immediately revert
an element's approval to `in-progress` at save. Two skills break this:

**comment-review.** I posted a comment on PS-CAC-001, ran "✦ Review with
analyst", and chose [I] Incorporate. The skill rewrote the "What happens"
block (and reset that block's provenance to `proposed`). Its own chat reply
said "you'll need to re-confirm it when you re-approve the element". But the
element's frontmatter still read:

    approval: approved
    approvalBy: M. Berger
    approvalDate: 2026-05-19

The content changed; the approval did not reopen.

**conflict-resolution.** Resolving the 6 callback-threshold conflicts ([D] on
all), the skill patched PS-CAC-005, CP-CAC-004, PG-CAC-001, TS-CAC-004 and
TD-CAC-005 (figures changed, block provenance reset to `proposed`). Its chat
reply again said "they'll need re-confirmation when you approve each element".
All six still showed `approval: approved` / `approvalBy: M. Berger` afterward.

Impact: in an audit-facing tool, elements display "Approved by M. Berger" over
content that was changed *after* that approval was given — a stale,
misleading approval stamp. Both skills clearly *intend* the element to reopen
(their messages say so) but neither calls `set_approval.py` after patching.

Likely fix: have `patch_element.py` revert approval to `in-progress` on every
write, or have each patch-based skill (comment-review, conflict-resolution)
call `set_approval.py` after editing. The foundational-run rework path *does*
reopen-then-reapprove correctly — only the patch-based skills miss it.
