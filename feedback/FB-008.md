Observation from comparing how the element UI reacts to chat activity across
the whole F2B run.

**What works well.** Once a chat turn completes, the document view reacts
correctly and promptly: element cards flip draft → "Approved", the left-nav
status dots go green, area fractions update (As-Is Process went 0/26 → 26/26),
new sections populate, and element-id references in chat messages render as
hover-cards. The wiring between the chat and the document state is sound.

**The limitation.** The refresh only happens at *turn boundaries*. During a
long single turn the view is completely static even though the wiki on disk
is changing under it:

- During the ~25-minute document-ingest turn, 38 element files were written
  to disk one by one, but the document view showed an empty process the whole
  time and only populated when the turn ended.
- During the batched foundational-run turn, elements were being approved on
  disk while their cards still showed the pre-turn state.

Functionally consistent with the "re-reads after each turn" design, but for
the longest operations it means the SME watches a frozen, seemingly-empty
wiki for many minutes (see FB-003). Worth considering: poll the process
folder, or stream element-written events, so cards can appear/flip live as a
long turn produces them. That would also make a long turn visibly *progress*
rather than just visibly *hang*.
