Processminer is an LLM-powered banking-process documentation engine (Claude API + skill orchestration → file-backed wiki). Outputs flow into bank-internal artifacts and, indirectly via Sparx / Confluence / EPM aggregation, into regulator-facing material. Under the bank's AI Governance framework and the EU AI Act, this footprint requires formal registration, independent validation, control-function sign-off, and ongoing monitoring — none of which the system has today.

✓ What we have

  • Per-heading provenance contract (HALLUCINATION-PLAN.md) — every output heading carries source / derived / proposed classification with evidence quotes.
  • Schema-validated writers — the only mutation path; bypasses are CI-prevented.
  • Human-in-the-loop approval gate (set_approval.py) — no element approved without explicit SME sign-off.
  • Wiki integrity contracts — Python + TypeScript conformance twins.
  • Run-time orchestrator — pure-data state machine that proposes, never executes.

✗ What we don't have

  • No entry in the bank's model inventory; no MRM tier classification.
  • No 2LoD validation report before production use.
  • No formal AI Act risk classification on file.
  • No central audit log of Claude API calls.
  • No documented bias / robustness / adversarial-input testing.
  • No documented DPIA for PII in raw-sources.
  • No incident-reporting playbook for AI-driven harm.
  • No vendor-risk assessment of Anthropic as a critical ICT third-party under DORA.

Regulatory landscape — what applies, by when

EU AI Act — Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

DateObligationsRelevance to Processminer
2025-02-02Article 4 (AI literacy) · Article 5 (prohibited practices)In force. Every user needs documented AI literacy training proportionate to their role.
2025-08-02GPAI provider obligations (Articles 51–55) · GPAI Code of PracticeAnthropic (Claude) is the GPAI provider. Processminer is a downstream deployer; Article 26 deployer obligations apply if high-risk.
2026-08-02High-risk AI system obligations (Articles 6–49)If classified high-risk under Annex III, the full Article 9–15 stack applies (risk mgmt, data governance, technical docs, logging, transparency, human oversight, robustness).
2027-08-02High-risk AI systems embedded in regulated productsLikely not applicable.
Initial risk classification (working assumption — must be formally confirmed by AI Governance Committee):
  • Most uses → limited-risk under Article 50. Internal process documentation; outputs flow to humans who decide. Transparency obligation applies.
  • Tips into high-risk (Annex III) if used for HR processes (§4), credit-scoring (§5b), critical infrastructure (§2), or producing material the bank submits as regulatory evidence (§6).
  • Phase 1 #1 below makes this formal.

Applies since 2025-01-17

Art. 28 ICT third-party risk (Anthropic register, pre-contractual assessment, concentration risk). Art. 17–23 incident management. Art. 24–27 resilience testing.

Suggested sequencing

  2. First quarter post-go-live: the 22 High items.
  3. First year: Medium items + first Internal Audit cycle (Phase 3 #6).
  4. Steady state: Low items + annual cadence on revalidation, bias testing, red-team, DPIA refresh.

Open questions for the AI Governance Committee

Decisions that gate sequencing. Must be resolved before the Blocker list can be costed precisely.

  1. High-risk classification under Annex III. Does Processminer's actual use envelope cross into HR-decision, credit-relevant, or regulator-evidence territory? Phase 1 #5–#8 are conditional on this.
  2. Bank MRM tier. Tier 2 vs Tier 3 changes Phase 2 #3 validation depth by an order of magnitude.
  3. PII envelope. Are raw-sources allowed to contain customer PII at all? If no, Phase 5 #1 becomes a hard intake filter rather than a classification step.
  4. Anthropic terms — zero retention + EU residency. What's currently contractually available? If neither can be guaranteed, Phase 7 #5 champion/challenger may become mandatory.
  5. GPAI Code of Practice signature status. Is Anthropic a signatory? Drives evidence the bank can rely on for Phase 1 #4.
  6. National supervisor notification thresholds. Has the bank triggered MaRisk AT 9 outsourcing notification by adopting Processminer? (Likely no for internal-only documentation; Legal to confirm.)
  7. Internal Audit cadence. First audit within 6 / 12 / 18 months — driven by tier classification and the first material incident (or its absence).
