## The risk
There is no published peer precedent for governing a fully-automated release-approval rail, so the STP compensating control (II-FR-008) must be designed from first principles. The bank cannot copy a proven model, and a regulator or auditor may still reject the approach after it is built.

## Likelihood & impact
Likelihood medium — supervisory expectations on automated controls are firming but unsettled. Impact high: if the design is rejected post-build, anchor 2 fails and the STP rail remains uncontrolled, exposing the bank to a material audit or regulatory finding.

## Mitigation
Engage the regulator and internal audit early on the proposed design rather than presenting it finished; anchor it explicitly to DORA's continuous-monitoring expectation; and build the control in stages so the approach can be validated before full commitment.
