## The decision
Close the STP control gap (CG-FR-001) by building a bespoke automated compensating control for the straight-through rail — real-time rules validation, post-release monitored sampling and an anomaly-hold — rather than extending human 4-eyes to STP items or merely rewording the control to disclose the carve-out.

## Options considered
- Extend human 4-eyes approval to every STP item
- Accept the carve-out and reword CP-FR-003 to disclose that STP releases get no human dual-control
- Build a bespoke automated compensating control for the STP rail

## Rationale
Extending human 4-eyes to STP would destroy the speed the STP path exists for; rewording the control discloses the gap but leaves the highest-severity risk unaddressed. A bespoke automated compensating control is the only option keeping STP fast and genuinely controlled — and it won despite no peer precedent (IR-FR-001) because an uncontrolled release rail is a material regulatory exposure.
