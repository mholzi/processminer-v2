## What it requires
A credit institution must not incur an exposure to a single client or group of connected clients that exceeds 25% of its Tier 1 capital, and must identify, monitor and report its large exposures.

## Why it applies
Each release is a drawdown that increases the bank's outstanding exposure to one corporate counterparty; large or repeated releases to the same client move that exposure toward the regulatory concentration limit.

## How it is met
The limit check (CP-FR-001) at PS-FR-002 tests the facility's own undrawn limit, not the firm-wide large-exposure limit; concentration monitoring sits with credit-risk reporting — to be confirmed with the SME.
