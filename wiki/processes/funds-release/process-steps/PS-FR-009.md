## What happens
After the release is confirmed and the workflow item closed, where the release has drawn the facility close to its limit or shown a pattern of repeated drawdowns, the released item is referred onward — first to Compliance for a final risk view on the client, then to the client's CRM or relationship manager — to consider whether the client's facility limit should be increased. The referral is informational: it opens a separate facility-amendment process and does not hold up the completed release.

## Inputs
- The confirmed and closed release item, with its facility ID and drawn amount
- The facility's current limit and remaining undrawn headroom
- The client's recent drawdown history on the facility

## Outputs
- A referral to Compliance for a final risk view on the client
- A referral to the client's CRM / relationship manager to consider a facility limit increase
- A note on the client record that a limit review was initiated

## Why it matters
Feeding completed releases back to Compliance and CRM turns routine release activity into a relationship signal, so a client repeatedly drawing to their facility limit is offered a higher limit rather than being constrained or repeatedly deferred.
