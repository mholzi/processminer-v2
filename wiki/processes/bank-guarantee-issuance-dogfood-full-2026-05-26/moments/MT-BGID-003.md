## The moment
The client learns the guarantee has been issued and the MT760 transmitted to the beneficiary's bank; their underlying commercial transaction can now proceed.

## Why it matters
This is the moment the client's purpose is fulfilled. A prompt confirmation positions the bank as a reliable trade-finance partner. A silent gap — no receipt from the beneficiary's bank — sours the final impression of an otherwise smooth process.

## Design implication
The issuance confirmation should include the guarantee reference, value date, and — where technically feasible — a SWIFT acknowledgement status from the beneficiary bank, so the client knows the instrument has been received without needing to contact their counterparty directly.
