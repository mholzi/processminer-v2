## What it requires
Where a guarantee is backed by eligible counter-guarantee or collateral, the institution must re-attribute the covered exposure from the applicant to the protection provider. The re-attributed exposure must comply with the 25% Tier 1 large-exposure limit for the provider's group.

## Why it applies
Bank guarantees issued with cash collateral or counter-guarantees trigger mandatory substitution under Article 403. Failure to re-attribute the exposure to the collateral provider understates concentration risk and misstates COREP LE reporting.

## How it is met
CP-BGID-004 (Collateral Block Confirmation) records collateral type and provider at issuance. This data feeds the bank's large-exposure system for mandatory COREP LE substitution reporting; the re-attributed exposure is monitored against the 25% Tier 1 limit.
