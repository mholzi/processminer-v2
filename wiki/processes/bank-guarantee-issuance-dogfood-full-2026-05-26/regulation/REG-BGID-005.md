## What it requires
An institution must not hold an exposure to a single client or group of connected clients exceeding 25% of Tier 1 capital. Bank guarantees count as off-balance-sheet exposures at full nominal value; the cap applies after eligible credit risk mitigation (Articles 399–403).

## Why it applies
Each guarantee issued creates an off-balance-sheet credit exposure to the applicant. If a corporate client's group receives multiple guarantees, aggregate exposure can breach the 25% Tier 1 limit, triggering a regulatory breach reportable to BaFin.

## How it is met
CP-BGID-003 (Facility Limit Check) enforces the client's approved credit line before issuance. The bank's large-exposure monitoring tracks aggregate exposure per client group and triggers COREP LE reporting to BaFin/Bundesbank quarterly under CRR Art. 394.
