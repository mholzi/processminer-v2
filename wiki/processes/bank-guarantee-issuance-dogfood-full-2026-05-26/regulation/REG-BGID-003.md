## What it requires
Financial guarantees are off-balance-sheet exposures under CRR Article 111; exposure value equals the nominal guarantee amount multiplied by a credit conversion factor (CCF). Credit-substitute guarantees carry a 100% CCF requiring full capital backing against the contingent credit exposure.

## Why it applies
Each guarantee issued creates a contingent credit exposure that must be included in the bank's risk-weighted asset calculation. The issuing bank bears credit risk until the guarantee expires or is called.

## How it is met
Facility utilisation is updated in the Trade Finance System at PS-BGID-006, recording the contingent exposure. Capital calculation against guarantee exposures is handled by the bank's risk management infrastructure (to be confirmed with SME).
