## The moment
A creditor receives a rejection code and short description but no remediation guidance. Unable to determine what to fix, they escalate out-of-band. A second rejection on resubmission compounds the loss of trust.

## Why it matters
This is the moment where trust is most decisively lost. The bank has said no without saying how — a response that signals the bank's operations and portal experience are not designed around the creditor's ability to self-serve effectively.

## Design implication
Reason-code-specific remediation guidance — shown inline in the rejection notification, or linked to a help article — would convert the rejection from a dead end into a self-service recovery path, eliminating the out-of-band RM escalation.
