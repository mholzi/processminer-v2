## The moment
The registration confirmation appears in the Creditor Portal — the mandate is active and the creditor can immediately schedule direct debit collections. After an opaque wait, the loop is closed and action is possible.

## Why it matters
This is the trust-won moment. The confirmation is clear, type-specific and unambiguous — it signals the bank has delivered. How much trust is restored depends on how much friction preceded it: a fast journey amplifies the positive; a rejection-heavy wait dampens it.

## Design implication
The confirmation is currently well-executed. Accelerating the overall journey — reducing SLA, adding inline rejection guidance, improving status transparency — raises the baseline so the confirmation lands with its full positive weight rather than as relief after unnecessary pain.
