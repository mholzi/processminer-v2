## Context
An event-driven estate has many more internal hops than the legacy monolithic flow. Each hop is an authentication and authorisation boundary. The existing IP-allowlist model does not scale to dozens of services and the regulator has signalled that PSD2 expects stronger inter-service controls.

## Decision
Every service-to-service call uses mutual TLS with short-lived certificates rotated by a central PKI. Channel-to-hub and partner-to-hub calls use OAuth2 client credentials with token introspection at the gateway.

## Alternatives considered
- IP allowlists plus a shared bearer token.
- mTLS everywhere including channel ingress.
- JWT-based service auth without mTLS.
- A service mesh's built-in identity (Istio / Linkerd).

## Consequences
- Certificate rotation must be automated end-to-end.
- Local development needs first-class mTLS support.
- Token introspection at the edge adds a small latency cost — measured at 4ms.
- Outage scenarios on the PKI become P1.
- Aligns with PSD2 and the upcoming DORA expectations.
