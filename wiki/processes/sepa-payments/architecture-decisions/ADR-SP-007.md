## Context
SCT Inst is a 24/7 product; an entire-region failure cannot translate into customer-visible downtime. The existing payment hub runs active-passive with a manual failover that has not been exercised end-to-end in production conditions.

## Decision
Deploy every new and modernised application active-active across EU-Frankfurt and EU-Amsterdam, with synchronous database replication for transactional state and async for the time-series store.

## Alternatives considered
- Stay active-passive; invest in faster, tested failover.
- Three regions active-active.
- Active-active for hub, active-passive for risk.
- Pilot light in the second region, hot only on failover.

## Consequences
- Double the always-on infrastructure cost.
- Sync replication adds a latency floor — must be measured against the SCT Inst SLA.
- Operational maturity gain — chaos engineering becomes routine.
- Regulatory data-residency rules constrain region choice.
- Geographic redundancy fits PSD2 supervisory expectations.
