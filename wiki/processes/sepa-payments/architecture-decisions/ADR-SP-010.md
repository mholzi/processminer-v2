## Context
The estate has accreted three tracing systems and two metrics stacks. Correlating a slow SCT Inst across the hub, risk engine and core today requires three dashboards and a war room. The SCT Inst SLA cannot be defended without single-pane-of-glass tracing.

## Decision
OpenTelemetry SDKs in every service; OTLP to a central collector; Grafana Tempo for traces, Mimir for metrics, Loki for logs. Vendor lock-in deliberately avoided in favour of an open standard.

## Alternatives considered
- Datadog APM end-to-end.
- Splunk Observability Cloud.
- Stay with the existing per-team tooling and federate dashboards.
- Build a thin in-house abstraction over the existing tools.

## Consequences
- One vendor-neutral standard; the bank can swap backends later if needed.
- Migration of existing tracing instrumentation is a meaningful workstream.
- Lower licence cost than the proprietary alternatives at the bank's volume.
- A learning curve for teams used to vendor SDKs.
- Critical for the SCT Inst latency budget to be enforceable.
