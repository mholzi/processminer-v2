## Context
The SEPA SCT Inst scheme requires a 10-second end-to-end execution. The bank can choose to commit to scheme minimum, or set an internal target tighter than the scheme rulebook to leave operational headroom for partial outages and peak hours.

## Decision
Commit to P99 ≤ 10s end-to-end including all internal hops, with internal SLAs sized so each component has a budgeted slice: validate 100ms, screen 200ms, route 50ms, book 1s, clear-and-confirm 8s of headroom.

## Alternatives considered
- Commit to scheme minimum only — no internal headroom.
- Commit to P99 ≤ 8s — tighter than scheme, costs more in active-active capacity.
- Tier the SLA by amount band (≤€500 stricter, > €500 looser).
- Commit only to median, not P99 — easier to hit but less customer-visible.

## Consequences
- Every component owner has a budgeted latency slice to hit.
- Performance regressions become first-class — must be caught at PR time.
- Capacity planning is now SLA-driven rather than peak-volume-driven.
- Slow-path retries can blow the budget — needs fast-fail with notification.
- SRE practice has to mature in lock-step.
