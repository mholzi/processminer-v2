## Context
Today the payment hub orchestrates with synchronous calls and overnight batch hand-offs to risk and core. SCT Inst's 10-second SLA leaves no headroom for a synchronous fan-out to three risk engines plus core. Customer notification arrives minutes late via batch.

## Decision
Kafka is the canonical event backbone. Components publish payment-state events and consume them; the hub orchestrates by event correlation, not by remote-procedure call where it can be avoided.

## Alternatives considered
- Stay synchronous and parallelise the risk calls.
- Use a service mesh with gRPC plus circuit breakers.
- Adopt a request-response message bus (IBM MQ) instead of Kafka.
- A hybrid where SCT Inst is event-driven but legacy SCT stays synchronous.

## Consequences
- Component teams need to invest in idempotency and exactly-once semantics.
- Observability — tracing across event boundaries — needs OpenTelemetry from day one.
- Local development gets harder without good tooling.
- Operational mental model shifts: outages look different in an async world.
- New monitoring playbooks needed for consumer lag.
