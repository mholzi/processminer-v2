## Context
Two vendor platforms can host consolidated sanctions, fraud and AML logic. Both are expensive, neither natively models SEPA SCT Inst flows, and both lock the bank into a multi-year change cycle dictated by the vendor's release schedule.

## Decision
Build the Real-Time Risk Engine in-house on a streaming platform. Buy the sanctions list data and fraud feature feeds from specialised vendors; build the orchestration, model serving and ops layer.

## Alternatives considered
- Buy Actimize and configure heavily for SEPA SCT Inst.
- Buy NICE for fraud, retain a separate sanctions vendor.
- Build everything including the feature pipeline.
- Outsource model development to a fintech specialist.

## Consequences
- Faster iteration on SEPA-specific models.
- Higher up-front build cost; needs sustained ML engineering capacity.
- Vendor lock-in avoided, but onus of platform reliability is fully on us.
- Lower run cost once steady-state.
- Hiring is the binding constraint — 6–9 month ramp.
