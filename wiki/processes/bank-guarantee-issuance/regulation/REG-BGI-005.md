## What it requires
Guarantee issuance as a credit decision requires organisational separation of Markt (front office) and Marktfolge (back office) up to management board level (BTO 1.1), dual-vote approval, and four-eyes process controls for each credit transaction (BTO 1.2.3).

## Why it applies
Deutsche Bank is a German credit institution supervised by BaFin and subject to MaRisk. Guarantee issuance creates a contingent credit liability on the client's facility and is a credit business transaction under BTO 1.

## How it is met
CP-BGI-001 implements dual-vote: the Trade Finance Officer (Markt) processes the application; the Trade Finance Manager (Marktfolge) approves. Guarantees above EUR 5 million additionally require Head of Trade Finance electronic co-sign enforced by the system.
