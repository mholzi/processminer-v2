## The benchmark
Industry research establishes digital self-service as the baseline expectation for commercial banking lifecycle events including account closure. Corporate clients conditioned by fintech and consumer banking experiences expect to initiate, track and complete closure through a digital channel without staff mediation. Banks relying on phone-only, branch-only, or paper-only closure journeys fall measurably below this bar.

## Relevance
Deutsche Bank's corporate account closure process currently has no client-facing digital initiation or status channel; clients depend on staff to log, progress and close the case, with no self-service visibility at any point. Peers offering even a hybrid digital/paper model (Commerzbank, HSBC) already exceed this benchmark; fintech challengers (Revolut, Monzo) have eliminated staff mediation entirely.

## Evidence
AML Right Source industry research finds that digital banking expectations have migrated fully to the commercial side: "financial institutions fall short, leaving business clients stuck in slow, manual onboarding journeys"—a finding equally applicable to offboarding.
