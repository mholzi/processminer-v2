## The benchmark
Current-generation Client Lifecycle Management (CLM) platforms—S&P Global CLM Pro, Appian, Fenergo, nCino—treat end-to-end transparency from initiation to closure as a standard feature. Expected capabilities include: automated pre-condition checks on obligations, client-facing status visibility, automated closure confirmation, final-statement delivery, and data-archival notification. Banks without these features operate below the current vendor baseline.

## Relevance
Deutsche Bank's closure process routes all progress through an internal workflow tool with no outward-facing client visibility layer. Meeting the CLM platform standard would require adding a client-facing status touchpoint at obligations clearance, compliance confirmation, and final closure—three moments currently invisible to the client—and automating the confirmation and statement delivery step.

## Evidence
S&P Global CLM Pro's product description confirms that CLM platforms now provide end-to-end transparency from contract initiation through account closure, including automated obligation checks and data archival notifications, as standard product features.
