## The trend
The EU Anti-Money Laundering Authority (AMLA) commenced operations on 1 July 2025 and will enforce the directly applicable AMLR from July 2027. The regulation sets uniform EU-wide standards for customer due diligence, beneficial ownership transparency, and record retention — replacing directive-based national variation with a single binding rulebook.

## Relevance
Account closure triggers record-keeping obligations that run for years beyond termination. The AMLR's mandatory record-retention standards and AMLA's Technical Standards (due 10 July 2026) directly govern how closed-account files must be archived, how long they must be kept, and how retrievable they must be — directly exposing this process's undocumented retention setup.

## Evidence
AMLA commenced operations 1 July 2025; AMLR directly enforceable from July 2027; AMLA Regulatory Technical Standards on group-wide controls due by 10 July 2026.
