## What it requires
Personal data must not be kept in identifiable form for longer than necessary for the purposes for which it was processed. Once mandatory retention periods under HGB, AO, and GwG expire, personal data must be deleted or anonymised.

## Why it applies
Corporate account files contain personal data of authorised signatories, beneficial owners, and contact persons. Account closure triggers a data lifecycle event; the bank retains data under legal obligation until retention periods expire, after which continued storage lacks a lawful basis under GDPR.

## How it is met
Files are archived per the record-retention schedule. A formal data deletion protocol covering removal of personal data once all applicable retention periods have expired is not evidenced in the current process and is to be confirmed with the SME.
