## The journey
Barclays offers no direct online route for corporate account closure; clients must use a branch, telephone, or post. Accounts with multi-signatory mandates are postal-only: all authorised signatories must sign a paper closure form mailed to the bank. Barclays provides up to five years of transaction history and dispatches statements within ten working days of closure.

## Relevance
Barclays's manual-only closure for complex corporate mandates mirrors Deutsche Bank's own paper-authorisation dependency, confirming this as a sector-wide pattern rather than a unique competitive disadvantage. However, both institutions still leave corporate clients without any digital status channel during the closure period, creating an experience gap against digital-first European peers such as ING.

## Evidence
Barclays's public help page explicitly states there is no direct online route to close a business account and that multi-signatory mandates require postal closure forms signed by all authorised signatories.
