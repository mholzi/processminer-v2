## What it requires
Art. 6 GwG imposes special clarification obligations on financial intermediaries when there is increased risk — requiring enhanced scrutiny of client relationships that present elevated money-laundering risk.

## Why it applies
High-risk clients and PEPs in the bank's book require scrutiny beyond the standard periodic review cadence. The special clarification obligation under Art. 6 is directly engaged whenever the risk-rating model assigns a High classification or a PEP flag is active.

## How it is met
High-risk and PEP cases are routed to mandatory 4-eyes FCO sign-off (Step 6). A structured evidence pack is required before close-out, satisfying the enhanced clarification obligation documented in §5.1 of the DTP.
