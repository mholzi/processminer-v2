## The gap
Whether a High-risk or PEP case escalates to the Financial Crime Officer (FCO) for 4-eyes sign-off is analyst-discretionary. There is no system routing rule that mandates FCO sign-off for these categories; the decision depends on which analyst is on duty and their individual judgment.

## Risk
High AML regulatory exposure. High-risk and PEP clients may be approved without the required 4-eyes sign-off, violating the ongoing-monitoring obligations under AMLD6 Art. 13(1)(d) and AMLO-FINMA §22/§23. Owner: FCO. Target close: Q3 2026 (interim policy) / Q1 2027 (system enforcement).

## Remediation
Two-track remediation. Interim policy update by Q3 2026 making FCO sign-off mandatory for High-risk, PEP, exit decisions and approve-with-conditions on regulated products.
