## Target description
Key onboarding controls are monitored continuously rather than tested periodically by sample. Account-configuration verification is a formal, named control with an owner and an automatically generated evidence trail. Product disclosure captures an explicit, recorded client acknowledgement that monitoring checks on every case. The post-onboarding quality check shifts from blind sampling toward exception review of what continuous monitoring has already flagged.

## What changes
- Account-configuration verification becomes a named control with an owner and an evidence trail
- Product disclosure captures a recorded client acknowledgement on every case
- Periodic, sample-based control testing is replaced by near-real-time automated monitoring
- The post-onboarding quality check focuses on exceptions monitoring surfaces rather than blind sampling

## Rationale
Two control gaps leave onboarding exposed: an undocumented verification step and a disclosure control rated only medium-effective for want of evidence. Continuous monitoring closes both by testing controls automatically and generating the audit trail today's process lacks.
