trigger: A corporate client submits a Bank Guarantee application through the Corporate Portal, or via a relationship manager who keys it into the portal on the client's behalf.
frequency: ~200 guarantees per month (steady-state; varies with corporate origination flow)
scopeIn: Issuance of a bank guarantee on behalf of a corporate client in favour of a third-party beneficiary, from application submission through to guarantee delivery and facility utilisation update.
scopeOut: Guarantee amendments, claims and cancellations (separate processes). Counter-guarantees, which use a bank limit facility structure and follow a separate workflow. Standby letters of credit, governed by ISP98 rather than URDG 758 and handled under the documentary credits process.
processInput: A complete Bank Guarantee application including beneficiary details, guarantee amount, currency, wording type (standard or bespoke), validity period and the underlying commercial contract reference.
processOutput: An executed bank guarantee transmitted to the beneficiary's bank via SWIFT; client facility utilisation updated in the Trade Finance System.

To issue a bank guarantee on behalf of a corporate client in favour of a third-party beneficiary, ensuring credit, compliance and legal requirements are met before the guarantee is transmitted via SWIFT and the client's facility is updated.
