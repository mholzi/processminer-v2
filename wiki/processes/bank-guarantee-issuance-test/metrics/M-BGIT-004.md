## Definition
The percentage of guarantee applications requiring an exception escalation outside the standard process path — facility-limit referral, bespoke-wording escalation to Legal, or enhanced approval above EUR 5 million.

## Current reading
Approximately 22% of applications trigger at least one exception escalation. The rate has been flat for the last twelve months, suggesting structural drivers rather than volume effects.

## Why it matters
Exception escalations are the primary source of cycle-time variance and client frustration. Reducing the rate below 15% — the target — requires addressing the upstream drivers: incomplete intake (PG-BGIT-004), missing facility pre-checks (PG-BGIT-001) and unstructured Legal handoffs (PG-BGIT-005).
