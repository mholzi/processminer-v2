## The risk
A real-time credit limit pre-check that returns a provisional 'likely approved' signal before formal credit review may cause clients and relationship managers to treat it as a commitment. If the full credit assessment subsequently declines or conditions the application, the discrepancy damages the relationship and may generate complaints or escalations.

## Likelihood & impact
Likelihood is moderate — provisional signals are easily miscommunicated, particularly through the RM channel. Impact is primarily reputational and relationship-level, rather than financial, but is concentrated on the bank's largest and most commercially sensitive clients.

## Mitigation
Label the pre-check result clearly as indicative only and not a credit commitment. Train relationship managers on the distinction, and include explicit non-commitment language in the portal UI and any automated communication that references the pre-check result.
